About the Project

The purpose of the project

The reason for developing Sched-Ease is to simplify academic scheduling by resolving issues like time-consuming manual processes, scheduling conflicts, inefficient resource management, and limited communication tools. By providing a centralized, accessible platform, it enhances coordination, fosters collaboration, and ensures efficient use of resources for students and lecturers.

Live VIVA Tracker

The Live Viva Tracker in a scheduling app is a feature designed to monitor and provide real-time updates on viva sessions as they progress. Here's a brief overview

  • Real-Time Status Updates: Displays the current state of the viva (e.g: "In Progress," "Waiting," or "Completed").
  • Participant Tracking: Tracks which participants (e.g: examiners) are currently active in the session.
  • Time Monitoring: Shows elapsed time for ongoing sessions and estimated remaining time for upcoming ones.
  • Notifications and Alerts: Sends updates or alerts for delays, session starts, or completions to participants and admins.

Educational Chat System

A chat system between students and lecturers in a scheduling app enables seamless communication before, during, and after viva sessions or meetings. Hereâ€™s a brief explanation of its core features.

  • Real-Time Messaging: Students and lecturers can send instant messages to discuss session details, ask questions, or share resources.
  • Session-Specific Chats: Chats can be linked to specific sessions or meetings, ensuring conversations are organized and context-driven.
  • Message History: The system retains chat history for future reference, allowing both parties to revisit important discussions or instructions.
  • File Sharing: Students and lecturers can share documents, presentations, or study materials directly within the chat interface.
  • Private and Group Chats: The chat system may support one-on-one conversations or group chats for broader discussions, such as with multiple students or examiners.

Voice to Text LogBook Generator

A Voice-to-Text Logbook Generator in a scheduling app converts spoken words into written text to create automatic logs or meeting notes. Here's a brief explanation

  • Voice Recognition: The system uses speech-to-text technology to transcribe spoken words into text, capturing meeting discussions, feedback, or session summaries.
  • Real-Time Logging: As the conversation progresses, the logbook is updated in real time, capturing every significant point or interaction.
  • Session-Specific Logs: Logs are linked to specific sessions or meetings, making it easy to associate conversations with their respective events or topics.
  • Editable Logs: After the transcription, users (students, lecturers, or admins) can edit the log for clarity, correct errors, or add additional comments.
